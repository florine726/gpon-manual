GPON Monitoring Bot
User Guide

// Diagnostic workflow for KONNECT staff agents //
Overview

The GPON Monitoring Bot is available in your Lark group chat. It allows KONNECT Staff agents to instantly retrieve network diagnostics for any customer — including building status, ONU/ONT status, RX signal power, and customer segment — without needing to escalate to TechOps first.

Use this guide to understand what the bot returns, how to interpret the results, and what action to take for each scenario.

How to Use the Bot
🤖
Trigger by Building Code
@GPON [BUILDING_CODE]
Use this to check the status of an entire building. Replace [BUILDING_CODE] with the customer's building code (e.g. KENKK05317).
📡
Trigger by ONU Serial Number
@GPON [ONU_SERIAL]
Use this to check a specific customer's ONU. Replace [ONU_SERIAL] with the ONU serial (e.g. AHDI:8E4570F0).
Frequently Asked Questions
Q1 What does the bot return when I check a building's status?

When you query a building code, the bot replies with a full building status report. Here is what each field means:

Building StatusONLINE 🟢 or OFFLINE 🔴
Region | CodeRegion name and building code
Building | ClusterBuilding name and cluster it belongs to
TimestampDate and time of the check
Online 🟢Count of ONUs currently online
Offline 🔴Count of ONUs currently offline
Archived 📦Count of archived/inactive ONUs

Each ONU in the building is also listed with its serial number, online/offline/archived status, RX power, segment, OLT, and port number.

📸 Bot Response — Building Status ONLINE
Q2 Building Status is ONLINE — what do I do next?

If the building is ONLINE 🟢, the issue is isolated to the customer's individual ONU. Proceed to check the specific ONU status using the customer's serial number.

✅ Building Online
The rest of the building has connectivity. The problem is likely on the customer's side.
➡️ Next Step
Query the customer's ONU serial:
@GPON [ONU_SERIAL]
Q3 Building Status is OFFLINE — what do I do?

If the building is OFFLINE 🔴, this is a cluster-level or infrastructure issue affecting all customers in that building. Do not troubleshoot the individual ONU.

🔴 Building Offline
The bot will show the actual count of Offline 🔴 ONUs. If any ONUs have been offline for more than 2 days, they will additionally appear as Archived 📦. Both can appear at the same time — e.g. Online: 0 · Offline: 5 · Archived: 3.
⚠️ Action Required
Escalate immediately to TechOps for a cluster check. Inform the customer that the issue is on the network side and is being investigated.
📸 Bot Response — Building Status OFFLINE
💡 Note: Archived ONUs within an offline building simply means those specific ONUs have been down for more than 2 days. The building outage is still the root cause — escalate to TechOps regardless of the Offline/Archived split.
Q4 What does the bot show when I check an ONU's status?

When you query an ONU serial, the bot returns a detailed individual ONU report:

ONU StatusONLINE 🟢 or OFFLINE 🔴
SerialONU serial number
RX PowerSignal strength in dBm
RegionRegion name
ClusterCluster name
BuildingBuilding name + building code
OLT | PortOLT number and port
SegmentVIP, VVIP, or Standard
Last SeenTimestamp when ONU last connected (if offline)
Q5 ONU Status is OFFLINE — what should I do?

If the ONU is OFFLINE 🔴, you need to create a ticket and note the Last Seen timestamp — this tells you exactly when the ONU went offline.

🔴 ONU Offline
Check the Last Seen field to determine how long the customer has been offline. Record this in the ticket.
🎫 Action Required
Create an ONU Ticket and note the Last Seen time. Then check the customer's segment (Step 4).
📸 Bot Response — ONU Status OFFLINE
💡 Tip: The Last Seen timestamp helps TechOps understand whether the outage is new or has been ongoing, which affects how urgently it is prioritised.
Q6 ONU Status is ONLINE — how do I interpret the RX Power?

If the ONU is ONLINE 🟢, check the RX Power (signal strength) value. The dBm reading tells you the quality of the signal and determines your next action:

RX Power Range Signal Interpretation Action
-8 to -27 dBm Signal level is abnormally strong — potential line fault or equipment issue ⚠️ Escalate to TechOps
-28 to -40 dBm Signal is weak — customer may experience slow speeds or intermittent drops 🎫 Create ONU Ticket
💡 Remember: Even though the ONU appears online, a poor or abnormal RX power reading means the customer will likely experience issues. Always act on the reading — do not dismiss the complaint just because the ONU shows as online.
Q7 What is the Segment field and why does it matter?

The Segment field shows the customer's service tier. It appears in both the building status list and the individual ONU report. It directly determines how you handle their ticket:

⭐ VIP / VVIP
High-priority customers. After creating a ticket, follow up and assign a technician immediately. These customers must be prioritised over standard clients.
📋 Standard
Regular customers. Create the ticket and follow the standard SLA process. No further escalation needed beyond the ticket.
💡 Note: VVIP and VIP customers should never be left on a ticket alone. Always confirm a technician has been assigned and follow up proactively.
Q8 What does "Archived" mean for an ONU?

An Archived 📦 ONU means the device has been offline for more than 2 days and has been moved to archived status on the network. The bot will display how many days it has been inactive.

📦 Archived ONU
This ONU has not been reachable for more than 2 days. It is different from a short-term offline ONU — it indicates a prolonged outage that needs attention.
➡️ Action
Create a ticket and note the Days Inactive. Escalate to TechOps for investigation. Check the segment — if VIP/VVIP, prioritise immediately.
💡 Note: Archived does not mean the customer has left. It simply means the ONU has been offline for more than 2 days. Always follow up with the customer and treat it as an active fault.
Q9 What is the full diagnostic flow from start to finish?

Follow this 4-step process every time a customer reports a connectivity issue:

Step 1Run the bot → Check Building Status
↳ OfflineEscalate to TechOps (cluster issue)
↳ OnlineProceed to Step 2
Step 2Check ONU Status (serial number)
↳ OfflineCreate ONU ticket + note Last Seen → Step 4
↳ OnlineProceed to Step 3
Step 3Check RX Power
↳ -8 to -27Escalate to TechOps
↳ -28 to -40Create ONU Ticket
Step 4Check Segment
↳ VIP/VVIPFollow up + assign technician (priority)
↳ StandardTicket created — standard SLA, no further action
Agent Tips & Rules
⭐ VVIP ESCALATION RULE
After creating the ONU ticket, agent must check if the client is VVIP. If yes, follow up directly with the assigned technician to ensure the client is prioritised immediately.
▶ LAST SEEN RULE
Always check the "Last Seen" timestamp when an ONU is offline to determine exactly when it dropped and assess the outage duration.
💡 TIP — "DEGRADED" STATUS
If you see the ONU status as "Degraded", this means the ONU has just gone offline or is in the process of dropping. Treat it the same as Offline and proceed with the ONU offline flow.
⚠️ TIP — "DATA MAY NOT BE ACCURATE"
If the bot output shows ⚠️ DATA MAY NOT BE ACCURATE ⚠️, this does not always appear but when it does — do not proceed with any actions. Escalate straight to TechOps on Duty to check the bot's data source before doing anything else.
💡 TIP — RX POWER MAY LOOK OK BUT ONU IS UNSTABLE
Sometimes the RX power reading may appear within normal range, but when escalated to TechOps, agents may be told the ONU has high loss. This is because some ONUs are unstable and their RX power fluctuates — it may look fine at the time of checking but still be causing issues. Always mention this to TechOps so they can verify and check the ONU stability.
