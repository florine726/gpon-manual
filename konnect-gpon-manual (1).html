GPON Monitoring Bot
User Guide

How to use the bot, interpret results, and take the right action.
Overview

The GPON Monitoring Bot is available in your Lark group chat. It allows KONNECT Staff agents to instantly retrieve network diagnostics for any customer — including ONU/ONT status, RX signal power, building status, cluster status, and customer details — without needing to escalate to TechOps first.

Use this guide to understand what the bot returns, how to interpret the results, and what action to take for each scenario.

How to Use the Bot
📱 Trigger by Client Number Primary
@GPON [CLIENT_NUMBER]

Tag the bot with a customer's phone number (e.g. 254790275765). The bot will look up the customer's ONU and return their full connection status, including Building Status and Cluster Status.

🏢 Trigger by Building Code
@GPON [BUILDING_CODE]

Use this to check the status of an entire building and view all ONUs and their RX power readings. Useful for confirming whether a fault is building-wide. Replace [BUILDING_CODE] with the building code (e.g. KENKK05317).

📡 Trigger by ONU Serial Number
@GPON [ONU_SERIAL]

Use this to check a specific ONU directly by serial number (e.g. HWTC:F31096B6).

Q2 What does the bot return when I check a building's status?

Querying a building code returns a full building report showing all ONUs in that building along with their RX power readings. Here is what each field means:

FieldDescription
Building StatusONLINE 🟢 or OFFLINE 🔴
Region | CodeRegion name and building code
Building | ClusterBuilding name and the cluster it belongs to
TimestampDate and time of the check
Online 🟢Count of ONUs currently online
Offline 🔴Count of ONUs currently offline
Archived 📦Count of archived/inactive ONUs

Each ONU is listed with its serial number, status, RX power, segment, OLT, and port. Use this view to identify whether a high LOS fault is isolated to one customer or affecting the entire building.

Tip: If you identify that the whole building is experiencing high LOS, tag TechOps to investigate the OLT port, cluster, or fibre infrastructure for that building.
Q3 What does RX Power tell me?

RX Power (Received Signal Power) is measured in dBm and indicates the strength of the optical signal at the ONU. Always note this value when diagnosing a fault — it tells you whether the issue is a physical/fibre fault or something else.

RX Power RangeSignal QualityMeaning
-8 to -24 dBm✅ NormalSignal within acceptable range
-25 to -27 dBm⚠️ WeakSignal degrading — monitor closely
Below -27 dBm❌ High LOSSignificant loss — likely a fibre or physical fault
Important: An ONU can be ONLINE but still have a critically low RX Power. Always check RX Power regardless of whether the ONU is online or offline.
Action Workflow — What to Do With the Results

Once you receive the bot response for a client, follow the workflow for the relevant scenario below.

SCENARIO A ONU is OFFLINE 🔴
1
Check Building Status & Cluster Status

Look at the two status lines at the bottom of the bot response. This tells you immediately whether the fault is isolated to the customer or part of a wider outage.

2
Building & Cluster = ONLINE 🟢 → Isolated fault

Raise a ticket for the ONU. In the ticket, include the RX Power value and indicate whether the ONU went offline with high LOS (below -27 dBm) or with a normal signal. This helps the field team determine the right type of investigation.

3
RX Power shows High LOS → GPON the building

Tag the bot with the building code (@GPON [BUILDING_CODE]) to check the RX power of all other ONUs in the building. If the entire building is showing high LOS, escalate to TechOps — the fault may be at the cluster, OLT port, or fibre level.

4
Building or Cluster = OFFLINE 🔴 → Wider outage

Do not ticket the individual ONU. Escalate to TechOps as a wider outage and inform the customer that their area is currently affected.

When ticketing an ONU: Always include the RX Power value and state clearly whether the ONU went offline with high LOS (below -27 dBm) or normal signal. This directly determines the type of field investigation required.
SCENARIO B ONU is ONLINE 🟢 — but customer reports a problem
1
Check RX Power

Even though the ONU is online, check whether the RX Power is within the acceptable range (-8 to -24 dBm). A degraded signal can cause intermittent issues even when the ONU is showing as online.

2
RX Power is normal (not critical) → Tag TechOps

The ONU and signal are fine. Tag the TechOps team for further investigation, clearly describing the problem the customer is experiencing (e.g. slow speeds, packet loss, intermittent drops). The fault is likely at the router, CPE, or configuration level.

3
RX Power is weak or below acceptable range → GPON the building

Tag the bot with the building code to check whether other ONUs in the same building are also showing degraded RX power. If the building-wide signal is poor, tag TechOps to investigate the OLT port, cluster, or fibre infrastructure.

Tip: When tagging TechOps, always include the customer name, contact number, building code, OLT/Port details, and a brief description of the reported issue. The more context you provide, the faster the issue can be resolved.
